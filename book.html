i.

Before the words —
before the world even —
there was just this:

a quiet
that did not know it was quiet,
because there was no other thing
yet to be otherwise.

It is hard to describe.
Language is something
the silence had to invent
much later,
in order to remember
where it came from.

ii.

Then —
somewhere that is also
no-where —
the silence broke open,
and a song began
that has not yet finished
singing.

The carbon in your fingerbones,
the iron in your blood,
the calcium humming in your teeth —

each one was made
inside a star
that had to die
so that you could,
one ordinary afternoon,
sit down across from a stranger
and be understood.

iii · the song
a city, in some arrangement
that means something to someone
iv · the strangers
iii.

Most lives go unread.
This is the great quiet sorrow
of being a person in a city.

You walk past someone
and never know
what they are carrying.
They walk past you
and never know
what you are carrying
either.

Sometimes, by accident,
the right kind of silence
opens
between two people
and everything
comes pouring out:

the grief, the small joys,
what they thought was beautiful
and what was broken.

There is no name
for this place.
It is older than language.
It is what we are,
underneath.

vi · the spiral
iv.

There are patterns
made to disappear.

In some places,
on some mornings,
people kneel down on stone
and press colored powder
into the shape of a flower
that will not last past evening.

By dusk, someone has walked
through it.
The flower is gone.
The making was the flower.

This is older
than anything we call art.
This is what the universe does,
on a longer schedule:
forms a thing exquisitely,
holds it for a while,
and then —
lets it go,
unbothered.

The disappearance
is the point.

viii · underneath
v.

At some point —
no one is sure when —
the universe opened
an eye.

The eye looked
and was startled to discover
it was the universe
looking at itself.

This is what you are.
This is what every you
has always been.

A way the cosmos
has found to grow a face
and ask:
what am I?

The asking is the point.
The asking is everything.

vi.

Water is a strange mirror.
It holds your face
and breaks your face
in the same moment.

Every reflection in water
is unstable.
Every reflection in water
is true.

To be human
is to fall in love
with what cannot stay.

This is the first sorrow.
It is also the architecture
of every joy
you have ever had.

In Japan there is an old practice
of mending broken bowls
with seams of gold.
The crack is not hidden.
It is offered light.

The bowl is more beautiful
for the breaking.

So is the world.
So are you.

xii · the imperfect mirror
vii.

Some rooms remember.

Not because they were made to,
but because everything
that has happened in a room
leaves a softness behind,
and softness, accumulated,
begins to feel
like welcome.

You did not arrive empty.
You have inherited
the small accumulated kindnesses
of everyone
who was there before you —
their patience,
their breath,
the residue of their attention.

You will leave something too.
Not a name.
Not a story.
A quality
the next person
will not be able to identify
but will quietly trust.

You are not in the world.
You are something
the world is doing.

xiii · inheritance
epilogue

Soon you will close this book.
Soon you will look up.

Whatever you see —
the wall, the window,
a face, your hands —

you will be looking at
the inside of the same thing
you have always been
the inside of.

go gently.
you have always been
bloom.
