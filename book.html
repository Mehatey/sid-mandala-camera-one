bloom

a small book
about very large things —
and the small ones
that are also large
·
siddharth mehta · 2026

"you are something the world is doing
in the same way that a wave
is something the ocean is doing."

— a thought, not yet your own —
i.

Before the words —
before the world even —
there was just this:

a quiet that did not know
it was quiet,
because there was no other thing
yet to be
otherwise.

It is hard to describe.
Language is a thing
the silence had to invent
much later,
in order to remember
where it came from.

i · the silence
a point, before everything
ii · the point
ii.

Then —
somehow, somewhere
that is also no-where —
the silence broke open.

A note. Then a chord.
Then a song
thirteen billion years long.

The carbon in your fingerbones,
the iron coloring your blood,
the calcium humming in your teeth —

each one was made
inside a star
that had to die
so that you could,
one ordinary morning,
brush your teeth.

iii · the singing
stars, in some arrangement
that means something to someone
iv · the stars
iii.

The universe
is fond of spirals.

Galaxies turn into them.
Hurricanes turn into them.
Snail shells, fern fronds,
the curl of a wave,
the curl of a cochlea —

all the same gesture
at different scales.

A pattern is just
the cosmos rehearsing
how to bloom
in front of itself.

v · the spiral
phyllotaxis · the angle
at which the universe likes to count
vi · the spiral
iv.

Somewhere,
in a shallow place
where light met water,
matter remembered
how to copy itself.

This was the first yes.
Not the angels-and-trumpets yes.
The slow yes.
The yes that mycelium whispers
under a forest floor
without anyone clapping.

Everything that has ever loved
anything —
the bee for the flower,
the moth for the moon,
you for whoever you are missing —

is the unbroken descendant
of that first soft yes.

vii · the first yes
a network of yeses
under everything that grows
viii · mycelium
v.

At some point —
no one is sure when —
the universe opened an eye.

The eye looked
and was startled to discover
it was the universe
looking at itself.

This is what you are.
This is what every you
has always been.

A way the cosmos
has found to grow a face
and ask:
what am I?

The asking is the point.
The asking is everything.

ix · the eye
"the cosmos
is also within us"
x · the eye
vi.

To be human
is to fall in love
with what cannot stay.

This is the first sorrow.
It is also the architecture
of every joy
you have ever had.

In Japan there is an old practice
of mending broken bowls
with seams of gold.
The crack is not hidden.
It is offered light.

The bowl is more beautiful
for the breaking.

So is the world.
So are you.

xi · kintsugi
the seam holds
more light than the bowl
xii · the breaking
vii.

The mandala is what
the universe draws
when it wants to remember
that everything begins
at the center
and ends at the center
and is happening
right now
in the middle.

Begin.
Begin.
Begin again.

The pattern always returns
to where it started,
having gone
everywhere.

xiii · return
epilogue

You are not in the world.
You are something the world is doing.

Soon you will close this book.
Soon you will look up.

Whatever you see —
the wall, the window,
a face, your hands —

you will be looking at
the inside of the same thing
you have always been
the inside of.

it has been very glad to meet you.
